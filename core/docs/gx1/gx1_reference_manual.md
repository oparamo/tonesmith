### Supplying power to this unit

﻿GX-1 Supplying power to this unit

### Supplying power to this unit

#### Installing the batteries

Install four alkaline batteries (AA, LR6) in the battery compartment located on the bottom of the unit.

- When turning the unit over, be careful so as to protect the buttons and knobs from damage. Also, handle the unit carefully; do not drop it.
- If you handle the battery improperly, you risk explosion and fluid leakage. Make sure that you carefully observe all of the items related to batteries that are listed in the “USING THE UNIT SAFELY” and “IMPORTANT NOTES” (“Read This First”) leaflets for proper use.
- We recommend that you keep batteries installed in the unit even though you’ll be powering it with the AC adaptor. That way, you’ll be able to continue a performance even if the cord of the AC adaptor gets accidentally disconnected from the unit.
- Batteries should always be installed or replaced before connecting any other devices. This way, you can prevent malfunction and damage.
- “BATTERY LOW” will appear on the display if the batteries are low. Replace the batteries with new ones.
#### Supplying power via the USB port (using USB bus power)

- Connect the commercially available USB Type-C® cable to a 5V USB AC adaptor or to the USB port on your computer.
- Connect your guitar to the INPUT jack on this unit, and turn on the power.

### Selecting a memory

﻿GX-1 Selecting a memory

### Selecting a memory

The GX-1 features many different effects.

A combination of effects and their settings is called a “memory”.

There are two types of memories on the GX-1: the 99 user memories (U01–U99), which can be overwritten; and the 99 preset memories (P01–P99), which can’t be overwritten.

- Open the “play screen”.
- You cannot switch memories while any screen other than the play screen is displayed. If another screen is shown, press the [EXIT] button to return to the play screen.
- Press the [É] [Ç] switches to switch memories.
- Press the [É] switch to recall the previous memory, and press the [Ç] switch to recall the next memory.
- When a memory is selected, the indicators of the effect buttons that are activated in the memory light up.
#### MEMO

You can also change memories by turning [1] knob below the display.

#### Making the effect sound (reverberations) carry over when you switch memories (carryover)

﻿GX-1 Making the effect sound (reverberations) carry over when you switch memories (carryover)

### Making the effect sound (reverberations) carry over when you switch memories (carryover)

The function that preserves the tail of an effect (such as delay or reverb) when you switch to a different effect is called “carryover”.

- Press the [EDIT] button.
- The effect chain screen is shown.
- Turn the [1] knob to select the MASTER block (MST).
- Press the [ENTER] button.
- The edit screen appears.
- Press the [å] [ä] buttons to move the cursor to the “CARRY OVER” row.
- Turn the [1] knob to set the CARRY OVER parameter to “ON”.
#### Conditions in which carryover is enabled

To enable the carryover of the delay or reverb (included in the memory that you used before switching) after you’ve switched to a different memory, make the following settings.

- On the preceding and on the following memories, configure the effect chain to use the same effect, and use the same arrangement. Set each effect type to be the same as well.
- On each memory, change the effect parameter settings and the on/off settings.
#### MEMO

The carryover sound, such as delay and reverb, changes according to the settings in the memory after switching. For a natural effect, we recommend that you use the same settings as the memory before switching.

Restoring the GX-1’s settings to their original factory settings is referred to as “Factory Reset”. You can restore all of the settings to their factory-set values, and you can also specify certain items to be reset.

## Looper

﻿GX-1 Looper

### Looper

You can record what you play (up to 38 seconds in mono, and up to 19 seconds in stereo) and make the unit play back the recorded section over and over, as a loop. You can also layer additional performances with the recording as it plays back (overdubbing).

This lets you create real-time backing performances on the fly.

- The looper turns on when you press both the [Ç] switch and the [CTL1] (C1) switch at the same time.
- Press both the [Ç] switch and the [CTL1] (C1) switch at the same time again to turn the looper off.
- The CTL1 (C1) settings stored in memory are ignored when the looper is on.
#### Additional information

- Loop playback level setting
- [CTL1] (C1) switch color

---

### Loop playback level setting

﻿GX-1 Loop playback level setting

### Loop playback level setting

If you set the playback level at 100 (default value), the volume of the performance and that of the loop playback will be identical.

If you set the playback level to a value lower than 100, the volume of the playback will be lower than that of the performance.

As a result, the sound of the performance won’t get buried by the loop playback sound, even if you record a multiple number of times.

- Press the [MENU] button.
- Press the [å] [ä] buttons to move the cursor to the “LOOP SETTING” row.
- Press the [2] knob.
- Turn the [1] knob to move the cursor to the LOOP LEVEL row.
- Turn the [3] knob to specify the “LOOP LEVEL” value.
