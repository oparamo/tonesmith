# How to Use This Document

Each patch targets the guitar character of a specific Bad Bunny song. Unlike the Gilmour library, these tones come from studio-produced pop and urbano recordings — the guitar is typically processed at the mix bus level in ways a guitar signal chain can't fully replicate. Treat these patches as close interpretations of each song's sonic character, not exact reproductions.

Every patch includes a Recommended Signal Order callout (amber highlighted box). Follow this when programming. Reordering effects is done via: [EDIT] → knob [1] to select block → press knob [1] to make movable → turn knob [1] to new position → press knob [1] to confirm.

DtMF has two patches (DTMF-1 and DTMF-2). Program them into consecutive memory slots and compare them directly — DTMF-1 is warmer and rounder, DTMF-2 uses the ENHANCER and a GEQ roll-off to approximate the lo-fi tape quality of the recording.

## General Observations Across This Library

Almost every patch in this library is clean — Bad Bunny's guitar-forward songs are defined by space, warmth and restraint rather than drive or distortion. Moscow Mule is the single exception, and even that patch uses the Blues OD at minimal drive.

TRNSPRNT amp model is used frequently here — more than in the Gilmour library. When the guitar's character should come entirely from effects rather than amp colouring, TRNSPRNT is the right choice.

The SHIMMER reverb appears on Ojitos Lindos. The DELUXE amp appears on DtMF and Un Coco — the two warmest, most intimate patches. ROOM S reverb appears on the three driest patches (Moscow Mule, Pitorro de Coco, Turista).

Turista is the sparsest patch in both this library and the Gilmour library. No delay, minimal reverb, near-zero compression. If the tone sounds too dry, resist the urge to add delay — the nakedness is the point.

# Ojitos Lindos

Un Verano Sin Ti (2022) ft. Bomba Estéreo  —  Patch OL-1

Goal: Dreamy, psychedelic clean guitar. The song fuses reggaeton, cumbia, indie pop and psychedelia, and the guitar sits in a hazy, reverb-soaked wash that feels both tropical and otherworldly. The SHIMMER reverb is the centrepiece of this patch.

Recommended Signal Order:  FX1 (Chorus) → AMP/CAB → NS → DELAY → SHIMMER REVERB

Note: OD/DS and FX2 are both OFF. The entire character comes from the Chorus and SHIMMER reverb — keep the amp completely clean and transparent.

| Block | Setting | Why |
| --- | --- | --- |
| AMP/CAB | TRNSPRNT, GAIN: 12, BASS: 48, MID: 45, TREB: 55, SP: 1×12", MIC: RIBON121 | TRNSPRNT is the flattest, most neutral amp on the unit. No character of its own — lets the reverb and chorus do all the work. Ribbon mic for warmth. |
| FX1 Chorus | STEREO, RATE: 20, DEPTH: 35, LEVEL: 60, PRE-DELAY: 5ms | Adds the dreamy, slightly liquid quality to the tone. Slow rate keeps it from sounding too obvious. |
| OD/DS | OFF | — |
| FX2 | OFF | — |
| NS | THRESHOLD: 18, RELEASE: 40 | Very light — just housekeeping. |
| DELAY | ANALOG, TIME: 400ms, FEEDBACK: 30, LEVEL: 48, HIGH CUT: FLAT | Warm, recessed repeats that sit under the shimmer. |
| REVERB | SHIMMER, TIME: 4.0s, TONE: -3, PRE-DELAY: 25ms, LEVEL: 55, PITCH: +12, PITCH LVL: 28 | SHIMMER with +12 pitch shift is the signature of this patch — the ascending pitch tail gives the psychedelic, airy quality. |

# DtMF (Debí Tomar Más Fotos)

Debí Tirar Más Fotos (2025)  —  Patch DTMF-1

Goal: Warm, nostalgic clean guitar. A plena and hip-hop track, the guitar is gentle and slightly melancholic — intimate, jíbaro-influenced. This version prioritises warmth and roundness. A good starting point before trying DTMF-2.

Recommended Signal Order:  FX1 (Compressor) → AMP/CAB → NS → DELAY → REVERB

Note: OD/DS and FX2 are OFF. Simplest chain in the library — just compressor, amp, and dark reverb.

| Block | Setting | Why |
| --- | --- | --- |
| AMP/CAB | DELUXE, GAIN: 14, BASS: 58, MID: 52, TREB: 50, SP: 1×12", MIC: RIBON121 | Deluxe Reverb for warmth and intimacy. Bass pushed (58) for a rounded, almost nylon-string body. Ribbon mic for maximum warmth and top-end roll-off. |
| FX1 Compressor | ORANGE, SUSTAIN: 35, ATTACK: 65, LEVEL: 55 | Orange Squeezer — lightest touch. Gently evens out picking without killing dynamics. High attack preserves the natural transient. |
| OD/DS | OFF | — |
| FX2 | OFF | — |
| NS | THRESHOLD: 20, RELEASE: 40 | — |
| DELAY | ANALOG, TIME: 360ms, FEEDBACK: 22, LEVEL: 38, HIGH CUT: 2kHz | Very dark, very subtle delay — almost subliminal. Just adds a sense of depth to the strumming. |
| REVERB | HALL M, TIME: 3.0s, TONE: -12, PRE-DELAY: 30ms, LEVEL: 42, DENSITY: 4, DIRECT: 100 | Darkest TONE setting in the library (-12). Should feel like a warm room, not a concert hall. Low density keeps it from sounding artificial. |

# DtMF (Debí Tomar Más Fotos)

Debí Tirar Más Fotos (2025)  —  Patch DTMF-2

Goal: Same song, but targeting the lo-fi tape warmth you can hear on the recording — a slightly muffled, saturated quality as if coming through old equipment. Uses the ENHANCER in FX1 and a rolled-off high EQ in FX2 to approximate tape saturation. Compare directly with DTMF-1.

Recommended Signal Order:  FX1 (Enhancer) → AMP/CAB → FX2 (High GEQ) → NS → DELAY → REVERB

Note: Note: True lo-fi tape saturation is a studio-level production treatment baked into the DtMF master. This patch gets close using the ENHANCER for subtle harmonic saturation and a high-frequency roll-off via GEQ, but a dedicated tape-emulation pedal in the chain would go further.

| Block | Setting | Why |
| --- | --- | --- |
| AMP/CAB | TRNSPRNT, GAIN: 8, BASS: 58, MID: 52, TREB: 50, SP: 1×12", MIC: RIBON121 | Switched to TRNSPRNT at very low gain so the ENHANCER and GEQ control all the colour — the amp adds nothing of its own. |
| FX1 Enhancer | SENS: 65, LEVEL: 58 | The ENHANCER emphasises attack transients and adds subtle harmonic content, approximating the way tape saturation adds presence without obvious distortion. |
| OD/DS | OFF | — |
| FX2 High GEQ | 250Hz: +4, 500Hz: +2, 1kHz: +3, 2kHz: -3, 4kHz: -6 | Boosts warmth in the low-mids, then rolls off the highs — gives the guitar that slightly muffled, tape-worn quality. 4kHz cut is the key. |
| NS | THRESHOLD: 20, RELEASE: 40 | — |
| DELAY | ANALOG, TIME: 360ms, FEEDBACK: 22, LEVEL: 38, HIGH CUT: 2kHz | Same as DTMF-1 — keep delay consistent between patches so you can compare them directly. |
| REVERB | HALL M, TIME: 3.0s, TONE: -12, PRE-DELAY: 30ms, LEVEL: 42, DENSITY: 4, DIRECT: 100 | Same as DTMF-1 — the reverb character is identical, isolating the ENHANCER + GEQ as the only variables. |

# Neverita

Un Verano Sin Ti (2022)  —  Patch NEV-1

Goal: Smooth, slightly bell-like chord tone. The song centres on Emaj7 and Bbmaj7 jazz voicings at 120 BPM. The guitar needs to be pristine and glassy to let those extended chords breathe — think a clean Fender twin with a hint of chorus.

Recommended Signal Order:  FX1 (Compressor) → AMP/CAB → FX2 (Chorus) → NS → DELAY → REVERB

Note: OD/DS is OFF. The compressor before the amp evens out chord voicings that would otherwise bloom unevenly.

| Block | Setting | Why |
| --- | --- | --- |
| AMP/CAB | TWIN, GAIN: 16, BASS: 50, MID: 42, TREB: 65, SP: ORIGINAL, MIC: CND87 | Fender Twin for the cleanest, most glassy response. Mids scooped slightly for a bell-like jazz tone. CND87 (Neumann U87) for a flat, open response. |
| FX1 Compressor | BOSS COMP, SUSTAIN: 45, ATTACK: 40, LEVEL: 60 | CS-3 model for an even, controlled feel that suits clean jazz chord voicings — notes sustain evenly without pumping. |
| OD/DS | OFF | — |
| FX2 Chorus | STEREO, RATE: 10, DEPTH: 20, LEVEL: 45, PRE-DELAY: 4ms | Extremely slow, subtle chorus — just enough to add warm, slightly liquid quality to the jazz chords. Not obvious. |
| NS | THRESHOLD: 22, RELEASE: 38 | — |
| DELAY | STANDARD, TIME: 320ms, FEEDBACK: 18, LEVEL: 35, HIGH CUT: 4kHz | STANDARD (not ANALOG) — brighter, crisper repeats suit the glassy jazz tone better than dark analog repeats. |
| REVERB | HALL S, TIME: 2.2s, TONE: -3, PRE-DELAY: 15ms, LEVEL: 38, DENSITY: 5, DIRECT: 100 | HALL S for clarity — the extended chord voicings need definition, not wash. |

# Moscow Mule

Un Verano Sin Ti (2022)  —  Patch MM-1

Goal: The most forward, punchy guitar tone in the Bad Bunny library. A reggaeton-synthwave fusion, the guitar riff is staccato and present — slightly gritty, rhythmically precise. The only patch in this library with any edge to the amp.

Recommended Signal Order:  FX1 (Compressor) → OD/DS (Blues OD) → AMP/CAB → NS → DELAY → REVERB

Note: Blues OD in OD/DS is at very low drive — acting as a light colouring agent, not a distortion. FX2 is OFF. NS is active and tighter than other patches — the staccato riff needs clean note separation.

| Block | Setting | Why |
| --- | --- | --- |
| AMP/CAB | BOUTIQUE, GAIN: 38, BASS: 52, MID: 58, TREB: 65, SP: 2×12", MIC: DYN57 | BOUTIQUE (crunch combo model) at moderate gain. Mid-forward for riff presence. 2×12" for warm but defined cab response. DYN57 (SM57) for punch. |
| FX1 Compressor | BOSS COMP, SUSTAIN: 55, ATTACK: 30, LEVEL: 62 | Faster attack than other patches — adds the snappy, punchy feel the riff needs. |
| OD/DS | BLUES OD, DRIVE: 18, TONE: +8, LEVEL: 65 | BD-2 model at very low drive. Just a hint of edge and warmth — not actual overdrive. Keeps it musical without going rock. |
| FX2 | OFF | — |
| NS | THRESHOLD: 40, RELEASE: 25 | Tighter gate than any other Bad Bunny patch — keeps the staccato riff articulation clean. |
| DELAY | STANDARD, TIME: 280ms, FEEDBACK: 20, LEVEL: 38, HIGH CUT: FLAT | Short, bright delay — punchy and rhythmic. Brightest HIGH CUT in the library. |
| REVERB | ROOM S, TIME: 1.4s, TONE: +3, PRE-DELAY: 8ms, LEVEL: 28, DENSITY: 6, DIRECT: 100 | ROOM S — tight and forward. This riff needs presence, not space. Brightest reverb TONE in the library (+3). |

# Pitorro de Coco

Debí Tirar Más Fotos (2025)  —  Patch PDC-1

Goal: A traditional jíbaro song interpolating Chuíto el de Bayamón's Música Jíbara Para Las Navidades. The guitar should evoke a Puerto Rican cuatro — the indigenous string instrument central to jíbaro music. Fingerpicked, slightly nasal in the mids, intimate and dry.

Recommended Signal Order:  FX1 (Compressor) → AMP/CAB → NS → DELAY → REVERB

Note: OD/DS and FX2 are OFF. The highest mid setting in the library (65) is the defining characteristic — it creates the nasal, twangy cuatro-adjacent tone.

| Block | Setting | Why |
| --- | --- | --- |
| AMP/CAB | TRNSPRNT, GAIN: 10, BASS: 45, MID: 65, TREB: 58, SP: ORIGINAL, MIC: CND451 | TRNSPRNT with mids pushed to 65 — highest mid setting in the library. Gives the slightly nasal, twangy character of a cuatro. CND451 (AKG C451B) for natural presence. |
| FX1 Compressor | ORANGE, SUSTAIN: 30, ATTACK: 70, LEVEL: 55 | Minimum compression. High attack (70) preserves the natural transient of each plucked note — this tone lives on picking dynamics. |
| OD/DS | OFF | — |
| FX2 | OFF | — |
| NS | THRESHOLD: 20, RELEASE: 50 | Very light, long release — let notes ring naturally. |
| DELAY | ANALOG, TIME: 240ms, FEEDBACK: 15, LEVEL: 28, HIGH CUT: 2.5kHz | Shortest delay time in the Bad Bunny library. Very dark, very low level — almost invisible. Just stops the tone feeling completely dry. |
| REVERB | ROOM S, TIME: 1.8s, TONE: -5, PRE-DELAY: 10ms, LEVEL: 32, DENSITY: 3, DIRECT: 100 | Small, natural room. Lowest density in the library (3). Should feel like a living room, not a reverb effect. |

# Un Coco

| FX2 Chorus | STEREO, RATE: 14, DEPTH: 25, LEVEL: 50, PRE-DELAY: 5ms | Very slow chorus — adds a gentle shimmer to extended jazz chords without making it obviously modulated. |

# TURiSTA

Debí Tirar Más Fotos (2025)  —  Patch TUR-1

Goal: The most stripped-back patch in this library — and possibly in both libraries. Described as a bolero and requinto style by guitarists covering it. Nothing but a delicate guitar strumming beneath Bad Bunny's solemn voice. No grand instrumentals, no heavy production. Every note must breathe.

Recommended Signal Order:  FX1 (Compressor) → AMP/CAB → NS → REVERB

Note: DELAY is completely OFF — the only patch in either library where delay is deliberately absent. Any delay would add artifice to what needs to feel completely live and unprocessed.

| Block | Setting | Why |
| --- | --- | --- |
| AMP/CAB | TRNSPRNT, GAIN: 8, BASS: 50, MID: 58, TREB: 52, SP: ORIGINAL, MIC: CND451 | Absolute minimum gain. TRNSPRNT is totally neutral. Mids slightly up (58) for the warm, intimate voice of bolero guitar. CND451 for natural, close-mic presence. |
| FX1 Compressor | ORANGE, SUSTAIN: 25, ATTACK: 75, LEVEL: 52 | Lowest sustain (25) and highest attack (75) of any patch in either library. Does almost nothing — just the faintest evening of dynamics. Must not kill the natural playing feel. |
| OD/DS | OFF | — |
| FX2 | OFF | — |
| NS | THRESHOLD: 18, RELEASE: 55 | Lowest threshold in the entire Bad Bunny library. Long release — absolutely nothing should cut a note short. |
| DELAY | OFF | Deliberately OFF. The only patch in either library with no delay. |
| REVERB | ROOM S, TIME: 1.5s, TONE: -3, PRE-DELAY: 8ms, LEVEL: 28, DENSITY: 3, DIRECT: 100 | Smallest reverb in the library. Should sound like a small room or close mic — not a reverb effect. DIRECT at 100 means the dry signal dominates entirely. |

# Effect Choices — Reference Guide

Why specific effects and amp models were chosen across this library.

| Effect / Model | Why it was chosen |
| --- | --- |
| TRNSPRNT amp | Flattest, most neutral amp on the unit. Used when the guitar colour should come entirely from effects, not the amp — Ojitos Lindos, DtMF lo-fi, Pitorro de Coco, Turista. |
| DELUXE amp | Fender Deluxe Reverb. Warm and intimate — smaller than the Twin. Used for the two most emotionally warm patches: DtMF and Un Coco. |
| TWIN amp | Fender Twin Reverb. Glassy and pristine. Used only for Neverita where the jazz chord voicings need maximum clarity. |
| BOUTIQUE amp | Crunch combo model. The only amp in this library with any edge. Used only for Moscow Mule. |
| ORANGE comp | Dan Armstrong Orange Squeezer. Gentlest compressor on the unit. Used for fingerpicked/delicate patches where touch sensitivity must be preserved (DtMF, Pitorro, Turista). |
| BOSS COMP | CS-3 model. More even and controlled than the Orange Squeezer. Used where chords need sustaining consistency (Neverita, Moscow Mule, Un Coco). |
| SHIMMER reverb | Used exclusively for Ojitos Lindos. The +12 pitch shift in the reverb tail creates the psychedelic, ascending quality of that song. |
| HALL M reverb | Warm, spacious hall. Used for the intimate, nostalgic patches — DtMF and Un Coco. |
| HALL S reverb | Clearer, more defined hall. Used only for Neverita — the chord voicings need definition not wash. |
| ROOM S reverb | Small, tight room. Used for the three driest patches — Moscow Mule (bright), Pitorro de Coco (small/natural), Turista (near-invisible). |
| Enhancer (FX1) | Used only on DTMF-2. Adds subtle harmonic saturation that approximates tape character without actual distortion. |
| High GEQ (FX2) | Used only on DTMF-2. Rolling off 2kHz and 4kHz gives the muffled, tape-worn quality of the lo-fi recording. |
| RIBON121 mic | Royer R-121. Darkest, warmest mic. Used on DtMF, Ojitos Lindos, Un Coco — the warmer patches. |
| CND451 mic | AKG C451B small condenser. Natural, present instrument sound. Used on Pitorro de Coco and Turista where the playing itself must be heard clearly. |
| CND87 mic | Neumann U87. Flat, open response. Used only on Neverita for the clearest, most hi-fi jazz clean tone. |
| DYN57 mic | Shure SM57. Forward, punchy. Used only on Moscow Mule — the only patch that needs that kind of presence. |
| BLUES OD | BOSS BD-2 model. Used only on Moscow Mule at Drive: 18 — barely any drive, just light colouring and warmth into the amp. |
| No delay | Turista is the only patch in either the Gilmour or Bad Bunny library with delay deliberately off. Any delay would break the naked intimacy of the bolero style. |
